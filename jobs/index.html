Postdoctoral research fellows

We are looking for Postdoctoral Research Fellows for research in novel machine learning and data science methods. Biomedical data involve rich interactions that span from the molecular level to the level of connections between diseases in a patient and to the societal level encompassing all human interactions. These interactions at different levels give rise to a bewildering degree of complexity, which is only likely to be fully understood through data-driven and computationally enabled study.

We investigate machine learning, focusing on new methods for learning and reasoning over rich interconnected data and on translation of these methods into solutions for biomedical problems.

This scientific approach not only opens up new avenues for understanding nature, analyzing health, and developing medicines to help people but can impact on the way predictive modeling is performed today at the fundamental level.

Among others, possible research projects include:

  • Representation learning for biomedical data in efforts to set sights on new frontiers in genomics, drug discovery, and precision health beyond classic neural networks on image and sequence data.
  • Network embedding methods in efforts to bridge the divide between basic science and patient data.
  • Fusion of diverse data into knowledge graphs in efforts to combine biomedical data in their broadest sense to reduce redundancy and uncertainty and make them amenable to analyses.
  • Next-generation algorithms for networks, focusing on large networks of interactions between biomedical entities and their applications to network biology and medicine.
  • Contextually adaptive AI in efforts to advance algorithms to train more with less data and reason about never-before-seen phenomena as algorithms encounter new patients, diseases, or cell types.

Qualifications

We seek highly-motivated candidate with strong research skills and background in machine learning and/or applications in bioinformatics and computational medicine. Candidates must have a Ph.D. or equivalent degree in a field relevant to biomedical data science, such as computer science, statistics, engineering, biomedical informatics or computational biology.

Strong programming skills and experience with large-scale data and deep learning frameworks are required.

How to apply

Submit your application with a letter indicating your interests and experience, a CV, names and email addresses of 2-3 references, and 2 of your best publications via email to Marinka.

We highly encourage applicants to include links to any software they have developed. The position is available immediately and can be renewed annually.

Harvard undergraduate & Masters students

On a rolling basis, we are looking for Harvard undergraduate and Masters students.

We take undergraduate and Masters students at all levels. However, excellent grades and/or prior experience in machine learning/AI is preferred. Prior research experience is a plus.

To allow researchers to be successful, we expect:

  • A minimum of 10 hours per week commitment to research during school year, and a minimum of 15 hours per week for Masters students.
  • Researchers to commit to at least one calendar year of research with the lab (ideally more), including staying for at least one summer.
  • Researchers to have some prior experience in ML, which may include online courses. We encourage all applicants and undergraduate researchers to self-study relevant coursework.

If you are a current or admitted Harvard undergraduate or MS student interested in research positions, please email Marinka. Include your CV, current academic status, and any past research experience or non-course ML-related projects you have done.

Visitors, interns, and short-term students

We generally prefer visitors to stay for at least 6 months in order to carry out a high-quality research project.

Because of the large email load that Marinka receives, she may not respond to all applicants. Please do not take this personally! We do review all applications!

Harvard is an Equal Opportunity/Affirmative Action Employer. Women and minorities are especially encouraged to apply.
